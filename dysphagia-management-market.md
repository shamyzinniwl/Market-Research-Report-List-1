<p><p>Dysphagia management encompasses treatments for two main types: high (oropharyngeal) dysphagia and low (esophageal) dysphagia. High dysphagia involves difficulty swallowing due to dysfunction in the throat, often addressed with therapy, dietary modifications, and specialized swallowing techniques. Low dysphagia pertains to issues in the esophagus, typically requiring interventions like medications, dilatation procedures, or surgical options to improve swallowing. The market for dysphagia management focuses on developing innovative products and therapies to enhance patient outcomes for both types.</p></p>
