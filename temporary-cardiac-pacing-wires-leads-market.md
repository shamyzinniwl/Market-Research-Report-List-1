<p><p>Temporary cardiac pacing wires and leads are essential for managing heart rhythm disorders. They come in two main types: unipolar and bipolar. Unipolar leads consist of a single electrode that delivers impulses to the heart, typically providing a simple setup and ease of use. In contrast, bipolar leads feature two electrodes, enhancing pacing precision and reducing the risk of complications such as muscle stimulation. The choice between these types depends on the clinical situation and desired pacing outcomes.</p></p>
