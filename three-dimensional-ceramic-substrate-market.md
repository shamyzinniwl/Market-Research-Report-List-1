<p><p>The three-dimensional ceramic substrate market includes two primary types: multi-layer sintering and direct bonding. Multi-layer sintering involves stacking and sintering multiple layers of ceramic materials to create complex structures, enhancing thermal and electrical performance. In contrast, direct bonding refers to the process of fusing ceramic substrates directly together without intermediate layers, resulting in stronger interfaces and improved reliability. Both methods cater to diverse applications in electronics, contributing to advancements in miniaturization and efficiency in electronic devices.</p></p>
