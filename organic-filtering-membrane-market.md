<p><p>The organic filtering membrane market comprises three main types: microfiltration, ultrafiltration, and nanofiltration. Microfiltration, with larger pore sizes, effectively removes suspended solids and some bacteria, making it suitable for water treatment and juice clarification. Ultrafiltration has smaller pores, allowing it to separate macromolecules and colloids, commonly used in dairy and pharmaceutical industries. Nanofiltration, with even smaller pores, selectively removes divalent ions and organic compounds, ideal for water softening and wastewater treatment applications, catering to various industrial needs.</p></p>
