<p><p>Purge and Trap Concentrator (PTC) is an analytical technique used primarily for the concentration and extraction of volatile organic compounds (VOCs) from various matrices, such as water and soil. In this method, a sample is purged with an inert gas, causing VOCs to be transferred from the sample matrix to a trap. After trapping, the compounds are desorbed and analyzed, typically using gas chromatography.</p><p>The PTC market is experiencing robust growth, driven by increasing environmental regulations and the rising need for air and water quality monitoring. Industries such as environmental testing, food safety, and pharmaceuticals are increasingly relying on PTC for efficient sample preparation and analysis.
