<p><p>The food emulsion stabilizer market comprises several key types, each serving to enhance texture and stability in food products. Xanthan gum adds viscosity and prevents separation; pectin is utilized for gelling and thickening, particularly in fruit-based items. Carboxymethyl cellulose acts as a thickener and emulsifier; gum acacia stabilizes emulsions and aids in fat dispersion. Starch contributes to texture and consistency, while other stabilizers include guar gum and locust bean gum, offering diverse functional benefits in food applications.</p></p>
