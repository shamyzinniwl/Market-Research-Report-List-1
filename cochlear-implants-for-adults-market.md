<p><p>The cochlear implants market for adults is primarily divided into two types: unilateral and binaural. Unilateral implants are designed for individuals with hearing loss in one ear, providing auditory input to enhance sound perception and speech understanding. Binaural implants involve placing devices in both ears, offering improved sound localization and a more natural listening experience. Binaural options are increasingly preferred for their significant advantages in clarity and balance, catering to adults seeking comprehensive auditory rehabilitation.</p></p>
