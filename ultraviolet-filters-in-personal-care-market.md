<p><p>Ultraviolet (UV) filters in the personal care market are classified into two main types: organic and inorganic filters. Organic UV filters, often derived from carbon-based compounds, absorb UV radiation and convert it into heat, which is then released from the skin. In contrast, inorganic UV filters, typically based on minerals like zinc oxide or titanium dioxide, physically block and reflect UV rays off the skin's surface. Both types are essential in formulating effective sunscreens and products for skin protection against harmful UV exposure.</p></p>
